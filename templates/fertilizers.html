Fertilizers

Urea

Urea is a fertilizer that provides plants with nitrogen, helping them grow stronger and healthier.

Read More
×

Advantages

  1. Promotes vigorous growth and higher yields.
  2. Cost-effective and readily available.
  3. Can be used for multiple crop types and soil conditions.

Disadvantages of Overuse:

  1. Can lead to nitrogen leaching, polluting groundwater.
  2. Excessive use can cause plant burn and reduced crop quality.
  3. May increase greenhouse gas emissions and contribute to environmental pollution.

28-28

28-28 fertilizer contains 28% nitrogen and 28% phosphorus, supporting strong leaf and root growth in plants.

Read More
×

Advantages

  1. Provides a balanced supply of nitrogen and phosphorus, essential for plant growth and development.
  2. Supports robust root development and overall plant health.
  3. Enhances early growth and improves yields.

Disadvantages of Overuse:

  1. Can cause nutrient imbalances if phosphorus accumulates in the soil.
  2. Excess application may lead to environmental issues such as runoff and water pollution.
  3. Overuse can contribute to soil acidity and may require additional lime to correct pH imbalance.

DAP

DAP is a fertilizer rich in phosphorus and nitrogen that enhances root development and plant growth.

Read More
×

Advantages

  1. Supplies essential nutrients (nitrogen and phosphorus) that promote strong root development and overall plant health.
  2. Enhances early plant growth and improves crop yields.
  3. Helps in the development of flowers and fruits.

Disadvantages of Overuse:

  1. Can lead to nutrient imbalances in the soil.
  2. Excess phosphorus can contribute to water pollution through runoff, causing algal blooms in water bodies.
  3. May result in soil acidification over time if used excessively.
