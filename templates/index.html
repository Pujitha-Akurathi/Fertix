Crop Stages

Germination

Germination is the initial stage where seeds sprout into seedlings, beginning the crop's life cycle. It requires optimal moisture, temperature, and soil quality for success.

Seedling

The seedling stage is when young plants emerge and develop their leaves, laying the foundation for growth. Proper water, light, and nutrient management are crucial for healthy development.

Vegetative

In this stage plant focuses on developing its stems, leaves, and root system, which are essential for photosynthesis. This stage is crucial for plant’s overall health and productivity.

Flowering

Flowering stage is when plants form flowers that will turn into fruits or seeds. The success of this stage determines the plant's ability to produce a healthy, bountiful harvest.

Pod Formation

Pod formation in crops like beans and peas is when flowers turn into seed-holding pods.Proper irrigation and nutrient management are vital for healthy, fully developed pods.

Maturation

Maturation stage is when fruits or seeds fully develop and reach their final size and quality, ready for harvest. Proper timing is key to ensure the best yield and crop quality.

Harvest

Harvesting is the final stage in the crop cycle,where mature crops are collected from the fields.The timing of the harvest is crucial,as it determines both the yield and quality of the produce.
